Mushabbak (Fritters)


Main Ingredients:
  • 2 cup of flour
  • 2 cup of corn stretch
  • 2 cups of water
  • 2 Tbsp. of dry instant yeast
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 Tbsp. of sugar
  • 0.5 tsp of ground cardamom
  • 1 liter (4 cups) of oil for frying
  • Squeeze Plastic bottle
Sugar Syrup (Atir) Ingredients:
  • 3 cups sugar
  • 1.5 cups water
  • 1 tsp. lemon juice
  • 1 tsp. orange blossom water
  • Red Food coloring (optional)

Directions:

Note: A soft squeeze plastic bottle with a small hole in the tip is used. Syrup may be divided into 2 parts and desired food coloring may be added to each.

  1. In a large bowl, using an eggbeater on low speed, gradually beat in flour, salt, and cornstarch, yeast, sugar and cardamom; mix until well combined.
  2. Pour in 2 cups of warm water and beat until mixture is smooth consistency like yogurt (add 1 tablespoon of water at a time if required) for 2-3 minutes. Cover and leave to rise for 1 hour in a warm spot. In the meantime, prepare the syrup.
  3. In a sauce pan, place the sugar and water over medium heat for 10 minutes. Add the lemon juice and rose water and bring to a boil and simmer for 10 more minutes. Turn off and let the syrup cool completely.
  4. Stir batter to deflate the bubbles and fill plastic bottle with batter. Heat oil to 375F; in heavy skillet for deep frying. Squeeze batter into oil, forming a round ring 2-3 inches in diameter, and filling the inside of the ring with the batter, forming a laced rosette. Depending on the size of the fryer you can make 4-5 rosettes at a time.
  5. Fry for about a minute on each side. You’re looking for the fritter to puff up and get stiff and golden then lift up and drain of all the oil; immediately dip into the syrup for 45 seconds seconds, then lift and place in a serving platter.
  6. I usually split my Attar” sugar syrup” in half. Leaving one plain and the other I add my desired food colour to a shade you like.
