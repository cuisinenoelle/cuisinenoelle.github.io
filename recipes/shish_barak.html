Shish Barak (Lebanese Meat Dumplings in Yogurt Stew)


For Filling:
  • Lean ground beef 500g
  • Finely chopped white onions 1 cup
  • Oil 1 tbsp
  • All spice 1 tsp
  • Cinnamon 1/2 tsp
  • Salt 2 tsp
  • Black pepper 1tsp
  • Ground cardamom 1/8 tsp
  • Toasted pine nuts 1/4 cup
For Yogurt Sauce:
  • Plain yogurt (I use the red Plain Yogurt Balkan Style by Astro) 2 liters
  • Water 4 cups
  • White Rice 1/2 cup
  • Oil 1 tsp
  • Garlic, crushed 3 cloves
  • Dried mint 1/2 tsp
  • Dried cilantro 1/2 tsp
  • Egg 1
  • Salt 2 tsp (or to taste)

For Dough (makes 40-60 dumplings depending on shape used to cut circles):
  • All-purpose flour 4 cups
  • Salt 1.25 tsp
  • Water 2-3 cups

Directions:

  1. For filling, saute onion with 1 tbsp. of oil for 1-2 minutes then add beef and cook until done. Then add the spices and the toasted pine nut, cooking 1-2 minutes and set aside to cool.
  2. In a bowl add flour, salt and mix. Then mix 2 cups warm water gradually and knead until ball forms.
  3. Place dough on flat surface that’s been lightly dusted with flour, and flatten into a large disk (1/8th of an inch). U don’t want a lot of flour on dumplings because when cooking it will thicken the yogurt sauce to fast before cooking.
  4. For small Shish Barak use a bottle lid (this will take time to shape them) (or small cookie cutter, which I use), cut the dough into of dough roughly 2X2 inches.
  5. Place some stuffing into the circle them close it to form a crescent making sure to seal the edges well.
  6. Fold the dough back around your finger and turn down the edges to form a tortellini.
  7. Arrange on a lightly oiled baking tray and bake for 15 minutes or until light golden in color.
  8. In a Large cooking pot, lightly saute garlic in 1 tsp of oil, turn heat to low and add yogurt, egg, rice, salt and water and whisk well to break up the egg. Cook low-medium heat till the mixture boils and the yogurt thickens( remember to always stir in same direction and never leave unattended because you don’t want yogurt to separate on you)
  9. Add the dried mint and then the gently add Shish Barak a couple at a time. Simmer for 10-15 minutes. Remove from heat.
  10. Serve the Shish Barak with the yogurt sauce in a bowl and garnish with fresh chopped cilantro and toasted pine nuts.

Note: Making Shish Barak can easily be done. The moms and I get together one afternoon at one house and making it a shish Barak day. Each bringing their own filling while I make enough dough for all. Dumplings can either be bought in your local Lebanese shop or made at home in larger patches and freezing the baked dumplings in portions. I prefer to make my own and freeze the extras. Assembling the dish is easy once the dumplings are ready, because the yogurt sauce can be easily prepared.
